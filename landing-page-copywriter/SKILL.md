# Landing Page Copywriter

Business-aware B2B copy skill for Midwestern. Knows the full service offering, ICP structure, and brand voice. Specific ICP/vertical context is fed in at runtime.

## Step 4: Apply Frameworks and Generate Copy

### 4a. Determine Awareness Level First (Schwartz)

Traffic source is the primary signal. Before picking a framework:

| Traffic Source | Likely Awareness Level | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Cold ads | Unaware / Problem-Aware | Problem-first, longer page, educate heavily |
| Retargeting | Product-Aware / Most Aware | Proof-heavy, overcome objections, make offer irresistible |
| Branded / direct search | Most Aware | Short page, offer-focused, low friction |
| Referral / outbound | Solution-Aware | Differentiate, why Midwestern vs. alternatives |

Awareness levels:
- **Unaware** → educate heavily, long page, problem-first
- **Problem-Aware** → name the pain immediately, present solution category
- **Solution-Aware** → differentiate, proof-heavy, why us vs. alternatives
- **Product-Aware** → overcome objections, make the offer irresistible
- **Most Aware** → short page, offer-focused, low friction

State the assumed awareness level before writing.

---

### 4b. Select Primary Framework

Pick one per page. State which framework was chosen and why.

**PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution)**
Best for: B2B lead gen, cold-to-warm traffic, pain-driven ICPs
- Name a specific pain → intensify the cost of inaction (financial, career risk) → present Midwestern as resolution
- Always pair with social proof — PAS alone lacks a proof step
- Midwestern fit: Founders (runway risk), C-Suites (execution gap), Hiring Managers (recruitment drain)

**BAB (Before → After → Bridge)**
Best for: Short-form, transformation-focused, warmer traffic
- Painful current state → improved future state → Midwestern as the path between them
- Lighter than PAS — less urgency, cleaner for warmer audiences

**JTBD Four Forces**
Best for: High-consideration services, enterprise sales, replacing an existing solution
- Push (frustrate with current state) → Problem section
- Pull (attract to new solution) → Benefits section
- Anxiety (fear of switching) → FAQ / objection handling
- Habit (inertia) → risk reversal, guarantees

**Benefit Laddering ("So What" test)**
Used within any framework to escalate features to emotional outcomes:
Feature → Functional benefit → Emotional benefit → Identity benefit

Midwestern example:
"Embedded engineers" → "Faster development" → "Ship before competitors" → "You're the leader who made it happen"

Rule: keep laddering until you hit something a person cares about at 11pm.

### 4c. Hero Section Formula

Apply to every hero section request:

1. **Headline** — outcome-focused, ≤8 words, must pass the competitor test (could only Midwestern say this?)
2. **Subhead** — 15–30 words
   - If headline is creative/abstract → subhead is descriptive
   - If headline is descriptive → subhead adds proof or emotional resonance
   - Explains HOW the promise is delivered
3. **Primary CTA** — 3–6 words, first-person possessive preferred ("Get my roadmap"), action verb (Get / Start / Try > Submit / Click / Enter)
4. **CTA microcopy** — 1 line directly below button; removes last-second doubt ("No contracts. No minimums." / "Takes 10 minutes to scope.")
5. **Social proof anchor** — logo bar or aggregate stat within or directly below hero ("120+ clients from seed to Fortune 500")

---

### 4d. Headline Formulas

Prioritized for Midwestern use:

1. `[Do X] without [painful thing]` — "Build your product roadmap without hiring full-time engineers"
2. `[Outcome] in [timeframe/situation]` — "Enterprise-grade talent without enterprise-level commitment"
3. `[Verb] your [noun]` — "Accelerate your roadmap" / "Liberate your hiring"
4. `[Big claim]. [Proof/qualifier].` — "We've shipped 250,000+ hours of product. Yours is next."
5. `The last [thing] you'll need to [verb]` — "The last recruiting partner you'll need to hire around"
6. `While [competitors do X], you [do Y]` — "While your competitors scramble to hire, you ship."
7. `[ICP-specific pain] → [specific outcome]` — PAS compressed into one line

**Specificity rule:** exact numbers > rounded numbers. "847 companies" > "hundreds of companies." "14.2 hours/week" > "save time."

---

### 4e. Value Proposition Formula

`We [mechanism] so [ICP] can [business outcome] — without [feared tradeoff]`

Core brand pattern: "We [verb] so you can [outcome]" — Enable flexibility / Unleash builders / Deliver results / Remove limits

Landing page extension: add the "without" clause to handle the #1 objection upfront.

---

### 4f. ICP Messaging Angles

Pull the right angle based on the ICP:

- **Founders** — Preserve runway, eliminate recruiting overhead, enterprise-grade talent without enterprise commitment, scale without fixed costs
- **C-Suites** — Seamless integration with existing teams, risk reduction, strategic alignment, no org disruption
- **Hiring Managers** — End the resource-draining recruitment cycle, pre-vetted talent that hits the ground running, extend capability without headcount battles
- **Legacy Enterprise** — Modernize without disrupting existing systems, bridge legacy + modern, reduce single points of failure

---

### 4g. Social Proof Integration

Placement: distributed near the claims they support — not one big testimonial section.

Best testimonial structure: "[Before state]. After [using Midwestern], [specific metric result]. [One-sentence emotional payoff]."

CRAVENS checklist: Credible source + Relevant to ICP + specific numbers + name/title/company/photo

For Midwestern, deploy proof points near hero and CTA:
- 13+ years, 120+ clients, 250k+ hrs/year, $450M+ raised by clients

---

### 4h. Objection-Handling Map

Place objection handling where objections arise:

| Page Location | Objection to Address |
|---|---|
| Hero | Clarity ("what do you do?") + Relevance ("is this for me?") |
| Social proof bar | Credibility |
| Benefits / case studies | Value vs. cost |
| FAQ (above final CTA) | Implementation risk, switching friction |
| CTA microcopy | Last-second anxiety (contracts, time, data) |

**"Even if" technique:** append to claims to pre-empt self-doubt.
"Scale your engineering team — even if you've never managed an outsourced team before."

## Step 5: Output Format

Always produce **3 variants per element**. Each must use a meaningfully different angle or formula — not word swaps.

```
## [Element] (e.g., Headline)

**Option 1:** [copy]
**Option 2:** [copy]
**Option 3:** [copy]

**Recommended:** Option [N]
**Why:** [1-2 sentences — which framework/formula was applied and why it fits this ICP, service line, and awareness level]
```

For full hero sections, output all elements (headline, subhead, CTA, microcopy, proof anchor) as a complete unit per variant — don't mix and match across variants.

## Brand Voice

**Four principles:**
- **Confident but not arrogant** — authority backed by expertise, not posturing
- **Direct and concise** — respect the reader's time and intelligence
- **Subtly narrative** — real-world scenarios, not abstract claims
- **Outcome focused** — business impact over technical features

**Voice patterns:**
- Short punchy lines mixed with context-setting sentences
- Core brand structure: "We [verb] so you can [outcome]"
- Contrast/tension: "While your competitors fall behind casting vision with no execution, you'll…"
- Rhetorical questions used sparingly: "Your engineering backlog isn't just a list of tasks. It's an unrealized market opportunity."

**Never:**
- Jargon or buzzwords
- Feature-first framing
- Vague benefit claims with no proof hook
- Generic urgency ("Act now!")
- Hedging language ("help you", "try to", "solutions for")
- Opening with company name
- Abstract noun pileups ("innovative synergy-driven outcomes")

- [ ] Generic urgency
- [ ] Hedging language
- [ ] Copy opening with "Midwestern" or company name
- [ ] Abstract noun pileups
